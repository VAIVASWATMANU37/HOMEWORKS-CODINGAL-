AB de Villiers, often referred to as "Mr. 360" for his ability to play shots all around the wicket, is a former South African cricketer known for his innovative and aggressive batting style. Born on February 17, 1984, in Pretoria, South Africa, de Villiers made his international debut in 2004 and quickly established himself as one of the most versatile and destructive batsmen in the modern era. He holds numerous records, including the fastest fifty, century, and 150 in One Day Internationals (ODIs). Over his illustrious career, he scored over 8,000 runs in both Test and ODI cricket, with a batting average of over fifty in both formats.

De Villiers was not only a prolific batsman but also a reliable wicketkeeper and an exceptional fielder. He captained the South African national team in all three formats of the game and was named the ICC ODI Player of the Year three times. Despite his retirement from international cricket in 2018, de Villiers continued to play in various T20 leagues around the world until he announced his retirement from all forms of cricket in 2021. His ability to adapt to different formats and his innovative shot-making have left an indelible mark on the cricketing world.
