Shri Ram, also known as Lord Rama, is one of the most revered deities in Hinduism. He is the central figure in the ancient Indian epic, the Ramayana, and is considered the seventh incarnation (avatar) of Lord Vishnu. Shri Ram is often depicted as a paragon of virtue, embodying qualities such as righteousness, honor, compassion, and loyalty. His life story highlights the importance of dharma (duty), sacrifice, and devotion to family and friends. Shri Ram's journey from his birth in Ayodhya, his exile in the forest, the abduction of his wife Sita by the demon king Ravana, and his eventual victory over Ravana, serves as an enduring symbol of the triumph of good over evil. His legacy continues to inspire millions of devotees worldwide, fostering the ideals of moral integrity and spiritual wisdom.
